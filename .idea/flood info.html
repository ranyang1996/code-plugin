Victoria flooding damage

Climate change can contribute to the increased likelihood of extreme weather events. In the late 1990s, the average (direct) annual damage (AAD) cost of flooding across Victoria was estimated to be approximately $130 million based on riverine type flooding. Some ten years later, the estimated AAD, including all forms of flooding across Victoria (but excluding potential climate change impacts) is closer to $450 million.

29 Nov 1934 – Melbourne, VIC

140mm of rain fell in Melbourne over a 48-hour period.To the east of Melbourne, in South Gippsland, 350mm fell over the same two-day period. The downpour resulted in landslides, evacuations and many submerged roads. Eighteen people drowned, with a further 18 killed by collapsing buildings and other dangers. More than 400 buildings were damaged in Melbourne and 6000 people were left homeless.
